Health NZ — Real Time Data

Good, timely health data is absolutely essential for the health of New Zealand. Data Ventures balances security, privacy and timeliness to create a new solution.

"we’re looking forward to connecting our many data environments into one nationally consistent information hub. The data will be secured and ready for analysis so we can track how the health system is performing and where we can improve."

— Te Whatu Ora Interim Head of Integration Stuart Bloomfield, nzdoctor.co.nz, Wednesday 26 July 2023

The Challenge

The COVID-19 pandemic demonstrated the importance of having near-real-time data in a highly changeable environment. Existing, more manual, monthly reporting cadences were no-longer fit for purpose and did not provide the Te Whatu Ora’s the ability to collaborate regionally as desired.

This challenge was exacerbated by the fact the 20 former DHBs, all had a variety of technological infrastructure and data maturity. In order to reduce the burden on the ex-DHBs, Te Whatu Ora tasked Data Ventures with creating a proof of concept that would enable near-real-time data sharing utilising existing infrastructure and create potential architectural patterns that could be scaled around the country. Health data also carries significant security and privacy implications, so any proof of concept would have to meet best practice measures before being operationalised.

The solution

Following an in depth discovery and stakeholder engagement process - it was clear that the formation of Te Whatu Ora provided an opportunity to source timely data as close to its source as possible. This proof of concept contributed to a larger Real-time Data Project being undertaken by Te Whatu Ora, as a demonstration of ways data can be drawn together from across the health system.

Data Ventures pioneered a data collation platform approach, rather than individual pipelines, which allowed for streaming of data from existing infrastructure. This took a creative and robust architectural approach, backed by a security focused implementation, producing a set of very useful findings for Te Whatu Ora - as well as a successful pattern.
